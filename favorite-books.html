  • Master and Margarita by Mikhail Bulgakov – A surreal blend of fantasy and political satire that remains thrilling and profound.
  • Pan by Knut Hamsun – An atmospheric novel capturing man's primal connection to nature and emotion.
  • My Struggle by Karl Ove Knausgaard – A raw, monumental exploration of daily life, memory, and identity.
  • The Birds by Tarjei Vesaas – A deeply touching story about innocence, misunderstanding, and silent suffering.
  • The Count of Monte Cristo by Alexandre Dumas – A timeless epic of revenge, justice, and redemption.
  • The Sailor Who Fell from Grace with the Sea by Yukio Mishima – A haunting meditation on innocence and cruelty.
  • Small Things Like These by Claire Keegan – A masterfully quiet yet powerful novella about courage and kindness.
  • Drive Your Plow Over the Bones of the Dead by Olga Tokarczuk – A mysterious, philosophical eco-thriller.
  • The Brothers Karamazov by Fyodor Dostoevsky – A profound investigation of faith, doubt, and family conflict.
  • That Hideous Strength by C.S. Lewis – A darkly imaginative tale blending science fiction and Christian philosophy.
